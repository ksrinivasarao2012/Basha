Once upon a time, in a quiet village tucked between two blue mountains, there lived a clockmaker named Thomas. Thomas was known for making clocks that didn't just tell time, but chimed with the sounds of forest birds. One evening, a mysterious traveler knocked on his door, carrying a broken golden pocketwatch. "Can you repair this?" the traveler asked in a low, gentle whisper. Thomas took the watch, opened its delicate gear cover, and smiled; he knew this was no ordinary watch, but one that could measure the longevity of memories.
